Appendix · Moat & Defensibility

Donna's moat compounds
with workflow data.

Cross-workflow
data density
Every case deepens a connected dataset linking how leads were qualified, what evidence was gathered, how long work actually took, and where revenue leaked. Point tools see one slice. Practice management records work after the fact. Donna captures the full operational exhaust — and it gets harder to replicate as more workflows run through us.
Switching costs
compound with surface area
The more workflows a firm runs through Donna, the more of the firm's operating memory lives inside it — intake histories, case structures, forensic baselines, time patterns, integrations. The deeper lock-in is operational: Donna sits inside sensitive, high-stakes workflows where disruption has real cost.
Compounding
domain expertise
Every firm teaches Donna how legal operations actually run by practice area and jurisdiction — what documents arrive in what order, which third parties delay things, what intake signals predict case quality, how long work truly takes. That knowledge gets encoded back into the product.
